Most businesses don't know their true marketing ROI. They *think* they do, but when we dig into their data, we usually find:

- **Double-counted conversions**
- **Unattributed revenue**
- **Vanity metrics hiding real problems**

Here's how to fix it.

## Step 1: Define What "ROI" Means for You

Before you track anything, you need clarity on what you're measuring.

### The Simple Formula

```
ROI = (Revenue Attributed to Marketing - Marketing Cost) / Marketing Cost × 100
```

But the *real* question is: what counts as marketing cost? Do you include:

- Salaries and agency fees?
- Software subscriptions?
- Content production costs?
- Overhead?

**Be consistent.** Pick a definition and stick with it.

## Step 2: Set Up Proper Attribution

### The Attribution Models

There's no single "right" attribution model — it depends on your business:

| Model | Best For | Caveat |
|-------|----------|--------|
| First Touch | Brand awareness campaigns | Ignored nurture efforts |
| Last Touch | Direct response | Undervalues top-of-funnel |
| Linear | B2B with long cycles | Assumes equal impact |
| Data-Driven | High-volume e-commerce | Requires lots of data |
| **Custom** | **Most businesses** | **Takes work to set up** |

### Our Recommendation

Use a **custom multi-touch attribution** model. It's the most accurate for most businesses:

```javascript
// Example: Weighted attribution logic
function calculateAttribution(touchpoints) {
  const weights = {
    first: 0.3,   // Awareness gets 30%
    middle: 0.2,  // Consideration gets 20% each
    last: 0.3     // Conversion gets 30%
  }
  // Apply weights to your touchpoints
}
```

> "Good attribution is better than perfect attribution. Don't let perfectionism stop you from starting."

## Step 3: Build Your Dashboard

### The Metrics That Matter

**Forget vanity metrics.** Track these instead:

1. **Cost per Acquired Customer (CAC)** — Total cost / new customers
2. **Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)** — Avg revenue per customer × retention period
3. **LTV:CAC Ratio** — Should be 3:1 or higher
4. **Payback Period** — Time to recover CAC
5. **Channel-Level ROAS** — Revenue per channel / cost per channel

## Step 4: Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Pitfall 1: Mixing Time Periods

Don't compare one month of spend against six months of attributed revenue. **Align your time windows.**

### Pitfall 2: Ignoring Organic & Earned

Just because you didn't pay for it directly doesn't mean it's free. Content marketing, SEO, and word-of-mouth all have costs (time, tools, salaries).

### Pitfall 3: Not Tracking What Happens *After* the Sale

Retention marketing is often more profitable than acquisition. **Track post-purchase behavior** — upsells, referrals, repeat purchases.

## The Minimum Viable Tracking Setup

If you're starting from zero, here's what you need:

1. **Google Analytics 4** — Free, essential for web traffic
2. **UTM Parameters** — Track source, medium, campaign, content
3. **CRM Integration** — Connect leads and revenue back to campaigns
4. **Monthly Reconciliation** — Compare platform data against actual revenue
