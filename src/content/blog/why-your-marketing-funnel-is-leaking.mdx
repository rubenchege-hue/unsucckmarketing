Every business has a leaky funnel. The question is: **how much revenue are you losing?**

After auditing 150+ marketing funnels across Kenya and beyond, we've identified the most common — and most costly — leaks that hold businesses back.

## The 80/20 Rule of Funnel Leaks

In our experience, 80% of revenue leakage comes from just 20% of potential causes. The challenge is identifying which 20% applies to *your* business.

Here are the leaks we see most frequently:

### 1. The Awareness-Interest Gap

Your ads and content get people to your site, but they leave within seconds. This usually means one of two things:

- **Your messaging promises something your landing page doesn't deliver**
- **Your value proposition isn't clear within 3 seconds**

> "If your bounce rate is above 70%, you're not attracting the right audience — or your landing page isn't delivering on the ad promise."

### 2. The Consideration Dead Zone

People browse, maybe add to cart, then… nothing. They vanish into what we call the *Consideration Dead Zone*.

**Fix this with:**
- Retargeting sequences that add value (not just "buy now")
- Comparison content that helps them decide
- Social proof at decision points

### 3. The Checkout Abandonment Black Hole

Average cart abandonment rates hover around **70-80%**. This isn't just about price — it's about friction.

| Cause | Impact | Fix |
|-------|--------|-----|
| Hidden costs | +35% abandonment | Show total upfront |
| Forced account creation | +24% abandonment | Guest checkout option |
| Slow page load | +7% per second | Optimize page speed |
| Limited payment options | +12% abandonment | Add M-Pesa, card, etc. |

## How to Find Your Leaks

The fastest way to identify your funnel leaks is a **data-driven audit**:

1. Map your entire funnel (awareness → interest → consideration → purchase → retention)
2. Measure conversion rates at every stage
3. Identify the biggest drop-offs
4. Prioritize fixes by revenue impact

## The Bottom Line

You don't need to fix everything at once. **Find the biggest leak, fix it, measure the impact, repeat.** That's how you go from leaking revenue to scaling growth.
